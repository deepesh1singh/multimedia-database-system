About the Painting

Artist Leonardo da Vinci
Year 1503–1506 (possibly worked on until 1517)
Medium Oil on a poplar wood panel
Dimensions 77 cm × 53 cm (30 in × 21 in)
Art Movement High Renaissance
Current Location Louvre Museum, Paris, France
Technique Sfumato, Chiaroscuro, Atmospheric Perspective, Glazing
Main Subject The portrait of a woman, believed to be Lisa Gherardini, the wife of Florentine merchant Francesco del Giocondo.

Description

The Mona Lisa is one of the most famous and enigmatic portraits in art history. The painting features a woman with an enigmatic smile, seated against a dreamlike landscape. Her gaze appears to follow the viewer, and her subtle expression adds to the mystery of her identity and emotions. The soft transitions of light and shadow enhance the naturalistic depiction of her face and hands. The background consists of winding rivers, distant mountains, and a misty atmosphere, demonstrating Leonardo's mastery of perspective. The painting’s subtle details, delicate brushwork, and the sitter’s intriguing expression have made it an iconic masterpiece of Renaissance art.
